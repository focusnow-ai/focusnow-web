## The Problem with Unprotected Tracking

Most productivity and time tracking tools work by monitoring your computer activity and storing that data on remote servers. While this enables features like cross-device sync and team dashboards, it comes with significant privacy concerns if the data isn't properly secured.

Your activity data reveals intimate details about your work habits, interests, and even personal life. Which applications you use, how long you spend on each task — all of this paints a detailed picture of your daily life.

## What Could Go Wrong?

When your productivity data isn't properly protected, several risks emerge:

- Data breaches can expose your complete activity history
- Companies may use your data for purposes you didn't agree to
- Third-party analytics services may have access to your information
- Changes in company ownership could change how your data is handled
- Government requests could force disclosure of your activity logs
