FocusNow is designed to disappear: you set it up once, and it quietly builds your focus picture in the background. Here is the full setup, step by step.

## 1. Download and install

Get the app from the [download page](/download). On a Mac, open the .dmg file and drag FocusNow into your Applications folder. On Windows, FocusNow installs through the Microsoft Store — one click, updates handled for you.

## 2. Sign in

Open FocusNow and sign in with your Google or Microsoft account. Your account keeps your data synced — encrypted in transit and at rest — so a new computer or a reinstall never means starting over.

## 3. Grant the accessibility permission (Mac)

macOS will ask you to allow FocusNow under System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility. This step scares people, so let's be exact about what it does:

- FocusNow reads only the **name of the active app**, the **window title**, and for browsers the **page address**.
- It never takes screenshots. It never records keystrokes. Never.
- Without this permission, macOS simply doesn't tell any app what's in the foreground — so tracking can't work.

Flip the toggle for FocusNow, and you're done. Windows doesn't need this step.

## 4. Let it run

That's the whole setup. The top bar shows a tracking dot and "Ready to focus" — FocusNow now records how you spend your screen time, about once every two seconds, entirely on its own. Close the window if you like; it keeps working in the background.

## 5. Check your Dashboard

After an hour or so of normal work, open the Dashboard. You'll see your first summary — "You've focused Xm of Ym active today" — plus your Focus Score, focus time, and a color-coded timeline of your day. Green is focus, blue is neutral, red is distraction.

Curious what those colors and scores actually mean? That's the next guide: [How Your Focus Score Works](/guide/focus-score).

## If something looks wrong

- **Timeline is empty:** on a Mac, re-check the Accessibility permission — it's the cause almost every time.
- **An app is in the wrong category:** open Activities, find the app card, and use the ⋯ menu → Change category.
- **Something private you don't want tracked:** Settings → Privacy lets you exclude any app or website from tracking entirely.
