Your Focus Score is one number that answers a simple question: **of the time you were actively working, how much was not spent in distracting apps?**

## The formula, exactly

Focus Score = (focus + neutral) ÷ (focus + neutral + distracted) × 100

That's the whole thing. No hidden factors, no penalties for switching apps, no bonus for completing sessions. If you were active for 100 minutes and 20 of them were in distracting apps, your score is 80%.

## The five states on your timeline

Every moment of your day lands in one of five states:

- **Focus (green):** apps categorized as productive work — your editor, your documents, your design tool.
- **Neutral (blue):** necessary but not deep work — email, calendars, most communication tools.
- **Distraction (red):** apps and sites categorized as distracting — social media, entertainment.
- **Idle (gray):** your computer is on but you haven't touched it for 5 minutes (adjustable in Settings → Focus).
- **Away (faded):** no activity for 15 minutes — you've likely left the desk. Idle and Away time don't count against your score at all.

## Make the categories match YOUR work

Here's the important part: no app is inherently "distracting." YouTube is a distraction for an accountant and raw material for a video editor. FocusNow ships with 16 built-in categories and AI-powered categorization, but **you have the final say**:

1. Open Activities, find any app or site, and use ⋯ → Change category.
2. Or go to Settings → Apps for per-app category overrides, sorted by your most-used.
3. You can even create custom categories (Settings → Apps → Custom Categories) and mark them as Productive, Neutral, or Distraction.

Spend five minutes on this in your first week. A score built on categories that match your reality is a score you can trust — and improve.

## How to actually improve the score

Don't chase the number — read it. Open Analytics and look at **Focus by Hour of Day**: most people discover 2-3 golden hours where focus comes easily. Protect those hours for your hardest work, and put a [Deep Work session](/guide/focus-sessions) on them. The score rises as a side effect of better decisions, not the other way around.
